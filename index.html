The Challenge

What I'm actually looking for

I'm not looking for people who can just solve LeetCode problems. Everyone can do that now.

I'm looking for the next generation of leaders. People who take ownership, solve real problems, and have the attitude that companies actually want to hire.

I don't look at your resume. Your college name doesn't matter here. What you present in the challenge IS your resume.

That's why this challenge is weighted 70% behavioral, 30% DSA.

Why 70% behavioral? Because every company I've worked with says the same thing: "We can teach skills. We can't teach attitude." DSA gets you past the filter. Behavioral gets you the offer.

70%

Behavioral

You'll be given real-world scenarios and asked to demonstrate how you think, lead, and solve problems.

You'll also document real situations from your own life where you've demonstrated leadership principles. Ownership, bias for action, learning from failure, delivering results.

Themes you'll be asked about: ownership, handling ambiguity, learning from failure, delivering under pressure.

Sample question: "Tell me about a time you had to complete a project with unclear requirements. What did you do?"

I'm evaluating your thinking and actions, not your English. Write in whatever language you're comfortable with. Clarity of thought matters, grammar doesn't.

Format: 4 questions, ~100 words each. Written responses only ‚Äî no video, no MCQs, no group discussion. 30 mins per question, 2 hours total. Just you, your laptop, and your answers.

30%

DSA

Timed coding problems testing data structures and algorithms fundamentals.

Format: 2 problems, 30 mins each, 1 hour total. Each problem has test cases to pass + a ~100 word written explanation of your approach. Difficulty: 1 medium, 1 medium-hard.

What to study: Don't just memorize LeetCode patterns. Understand why algorithms work. Know your data structures deeply.

Example question: "What data structure would you use for Kruskal's algorithm and why? How would you optimize it?"

I'm testing if you understand trade-offs, not if you've memorized solutions. Know your arrays, trees, graphs, heaps, and hash maps inside out.

Duration: 3 hours
Evaluation: Behavioral reviewed using Amazon's Leadership Principles framework. DSA auto-graded.
